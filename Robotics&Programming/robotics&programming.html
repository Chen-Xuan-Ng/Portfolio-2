  • First Lego League 2020-2021

    This was my First First Lego League.
    To increase efficiency, our coach suggested we split into two groups and focus on different mission in different sections with two robots, which we did. Through this competition, I learnt of a way to make full use of manpower.
    However as this requires 2x the resources*, which the school/centre may not have, I rarely got the chance to do so.
    *Such as having to have a 2nd EV3 brick and double the parts to make the exact replica of the robot. Also, we would have to merge the programs when we were all done which would be a complicated process

  • IDE Series Robotics 2021

    My favourite part of the robot is when it uses its colour sensor to detect if the cube is red or green to accordingly push it into its colour-box (1:05). Apart from that, I am also proud of how smooth the motions were.
    However, one thing that keeps bothering me when I watch the video is how risky the turns were. I could have used the colour sensors to turn (e.g. Right turns: Turn until right sensor detects black). Also, I could have sped up the robot which would may have led to a 1st place instead of 2nd.
    The biggest challenge I faced programming this occured on the day itself. I recall the laptop crashed with the code and we had to re-program the entire code. Fortunately, we did it in time.

  • First Lego League 2021-2022

  • IDE Series Robotics Open Category 2022

    The robot had a fork to lift up the "human" by the arms to bring it to other locations. This was to prevent the huamn from being slanted when place down, which would incur a penalty. The robot used double sensor line tracing and p(roportional)-line tracing to complete missions. One risky part was where it had to make a sharp turn using p-line tracing, which was risky as if the robot turned too much it would go off-course.

    Our team experimenting with using double-sensor line tracing to make that turn, but eventually decided against it as double-sensor line tracing would be too inaccurate for us to accurately place the "human" in the box

  • IDE Code Hacker Secondary (Python)

    Received 21st/62

    I think I did pretty well for my age, but I still wish I had done one thing: Prioritise the missions. After the competition had ended, out of curiosity, I checked on the rest of the questions I had not done. To my utter disbelief, there were questions that I could have answered that were 3x the points of those I had been working on. Through this competition, I learnt to prioritise my missions. I did not feel intimidated despite knowing I was up against secondary school students as I had already competed against secondary school students in my FLL 2021-2022 and I had already done this before in my Code Hacker 2022. Also I was confident of my python skills
